Part 1 · What you're actually selling

The one-sentence definition

AETHER builds custom AI employees that handle specific business workflows end-to-end — autonomously, 24/7, for less than the cost of a part-time hire.

That's it. If you remember nothing else, remember that sentence. It's a "who do I hire?" conversation, not a "what software should I buy?" conversation. Position it as staffing, not SaaS.

The three truths that make AETHER different

1. It's not a chatbot. It's an agent. A chatbot replies to messages. An agent decides what to do and then does it. It can read a form submission, look up the prospect in your CRM, check three calendars, send an email, book a meeting, log it all, and Slack the right rep — without a human in the loop. That's a categorically different product than ChatGPT or Intercom.

2. It's custom, not templated. Off-the-shelf AI products (Drift, Intercom Fin, HubSpot Breeze) are one-size-fits-all. We build each agent around the client's actual workflow, their actual tools, their actual tone of voice, and their actual rules. That's why we can charge $2,997+/mo while they charge $99.

3. It's delivered, not sold. Most AI startups hand the client a login and wish them luck. We onboard them, build their agent, integrate with their tools, monitor it, and tune it monthly. We're a service business with software-level margins.

Three things to NEVER promise

Never: "It can do anything." It can't. It can do specific defined workflows extremely well. Vagueness kills deals at the discovery stage.
Never: "It will replace your staff." It augments staff. Position it as freeing up senior people from junior work, not firing them. This matters legally and emotionally.
Never: "It's 100% accurate." All AI models occasionally hallucinate. We mitigate with guardrails, knowledge bases, and human-in-the-loop on critical actions. Honesty here builds trust — overselling here ends careers.

Part 2 · How AETHER actually works

You don't need to be technical to sell this. But you do need to understand the stack well enough to answer the inevitable "so how does this actually work?" without bluffing. Here's the whole thing in plain English.

The five components

The Brain

Claude (Anthropic)

The reasoning engine. We use Claude Opus 4.8 for hard thinking (planning, judgment calls) and Claude Sonnet 4.6 for routine turns (drafting an email). Frontier-grade — same model class as the largest enterprises in the world use.

The Body

n8n (the runtime)

Where the agent actually runs. n8n receives the trigger (form-fill, missed call, incoming email), calls Claude, executes whatever tools Claude asks for, loops until done. Think of it as the central nervous system.

The Hands

Pipedream + Zapier

The connectors that let the agent act on the world. Pipedream handles secure OAuth into client tools (Gmail, Calendar, CRM). Zapier covers the long tail — 8,000+ apps. Between them: anything a client uses, we connect to.

The Memory

Supabase + pgvector

Where the agent remembers. Two parts: a structured database of every action it's taken, and a vector store of the client's knowledge base (case studies, FAQs, pricing rules, brand voice docs). The agent searches this on every turn so it never sounds generic.

The Eyes

Langfuse

Observability. Every decision the agent makes, every tool it calls, every prompt it sees — all logged. So we can debug, audit, and prove to the client what the agent did and why. Mandatory for any regulated industry (legal, medical, financial).

How a single turn actually flows (memorize this)

  1. Trigger fires. A prospect fills out the client's contact form → webhook hits n8n.
  2. n8n loads context. Pulls the agent's system prompt (its "job description"), the conversation history from Supabase, and any relevant knowledge-base entries.
  3. n8n calls Claude. Sends all of the above plus the new message. Claude reads it and responds with either a message or a tool call (e.g. "I want to call calendar.list_slots(window=7)").
  4. n8n executes the tool. Hits the right connector (Google Calendar via Pipedream), gets the result, sends it back to Claude.
  5. Claude decides what's next. Maybe call another tool. Maybe respond to the prospect. Maybe escalate to a human via Slack.
  6. Loop until done. Every step gets logged to Langfuse. Final state persisted to Supabase.

That's the entire system. Every agent we sell follows this loop. The only thing that changes per client is the system prompt, the tools available, and the knowledge base.

Why this stack is structurally powerful

The reason this works — and the reason a $99/mo SaaS competitor can't beat it — is that we own the orchestration layer. Drift owns chat. Intercom owns support. HubSpot owns CRM. But none of them own the decision-making across all those tools. We do. We're not selling a feature in someone's app — we're selling an employee that uses all of those apps.

Use this analogy on calls: "Salesforce is the office. Slack is the phone. Gmail is the mailroom. AETHER is the employee who walks between all of them and gets things done."

What it CAN do

  • Respond to any digital input (email, chat, SMS, form, webhook) in under 2 minutes, 24/7
  • Make decisions based on multi-step reasoning (qualify a lead, triage a ticket, route a document)
  • Use any tool with an API — book calendars, update CRMs, send messages, query databases, draft documents
  • Maintain long-term memory of every customer interaction and use it to personalize future ones
  • Escalate to a human the moment it's outside its scope, with full conversation context
  • Run continuously without breaks, vacations, or sick days

What it CAN'T do (be honest about this on every call)

  • Make physical phone calls reliably yet — voice agents exist but quality is inconsistent. We can handle inbound voice via transcription and text reply, but outbound cold-calling is human work for now.
  • Provide regulated advice (legal opinions, medical diagnoses, specific financial recommendations). We can intake, qualify, schedule, and follow up around all of these — but the licensed human makes the regulated call.
  • Move money or sign contracts without a human approval step. We design this in deliberately.
  • Replace deep relationship work — complex negotiation, high-stakes closes, sensitive client moments.
  • Work well with bad data. If a client's CRM is a mess, the agent's output is a mess. Sometimes the first phase of a deployment is helping them clean their data.

Part 3 · The cold-call framework

This framework closes a 15-minute strategy call from cold dial roughly 1 in 8 times when the list is targeted and the research is done. That's a strong ratio for a $3K–$8K/mo product.

Before you dial

  • Pick a vertical, not a list. Don't call "small businesses." Call "personal-injury law firms in Phoenix with 3–15 attorneys." Specificity = research-ability = relevance = answers.
  • Spend 90 seconds on each prospect. Their website, their Google reviews (look for service complaints), their LinkedIn headcount, recent press. You need one researched fact to drop in the opener.
  • Know one number cold. The vertical's specific pain stat (e.g. "law firms lose 42% of inbound leads to slow follow-up — that's a Clio benchmark"). Numbers separate you from every other cold caller.
  • Call between 8–10am or 4–5pm local time. Decision-makers are at their desks, gatekeepers aren't.

The opener (12 seconds, no improvisation)

Hi [Name], this is Jaden Green with GreenAI Solutions. I'll be straight with you — this is a cold call. Mind if I take 27 seconds to explain why I called, and then you tell me if it's worth more time?

Why this works: Cold-call defenses fire when they detect a script. You disarm them by naming the call. "27 seconds" is more believable than "30." The permission-ask reverses control — they're now in charge, which lowers their guard. Roughly 70% will say yes.

The hook (the 27 seconds)

Three sentences. Pain, solution, why-you-specifically.

I work with [vertical] in [region] who are losing [specific number] of [hours/leads/dollars] every week because [specific pain]. We build custom AI agents that handle that work autonomously — clients see [specific outcome] within 30 days, no extra hires. The reason I called you specifically is [one researched fact]. Worth a 15-minute call this week to see if it'd apply to you?

Notice what's not there: no "we're the leading provider," no "AI-powered next-gen platform," no buzzwords. Numbers and outcomes only.

Two qualifying questions before you pitch further

If they engage, don't launch into the pitch. Ask two questions — they tell you whether this prospect is worth your time, and the answers become your pitch material.

  1. "Out of curiosity — when a new [lead/inquiry/ticket] hits your business today, who handles the first response and how fast?" (Reveals the pain.)
  2. "If you could wave a wand and have one repetitive thing in your business just… handled, what would it be?" (Reveals the wedge agent to pitch.)

The close (you're booking a strategy call, not a sale)

Based on what you just told me, I think there's a 50/50 chance this is a fit. The way to find out is a 15-minute call where I walk you through exactly what we'd build for [firm name] and what it'd cost. If it's not obvious value in the first five minutes, you'll know. I have Thursday at 10 or Friday at 2 — which works?

Always offer two specific times. "When works for you?" is open-ended and dies. Two options forces a binary decision.

Part 4 · Objection handlers

Memorize these. Word-for-word. The fluency matters more than the cleverness — hesitation reads as weakness on a cold call.

"How much does it cost?"
"Depends on what we build for you — anywhere from $997/mo for a single templated agent to about $8,000/mo for an orchestrated fleet doing multiple jobs. Most clients land in the $3K range. But honestly the price isn't the question — the question is whether it'd actually solve the thing that's costing you money. Worth 15 minutes to figure out which one?"
"We already use ChatGPT."
"Great — then you already know AI can do the thinking. What we build is the second half: the part that actually does the work after the thinking. ChatGPT writes the email; an AETHER agent writes the email, sends it from your Gmail, logs it in your CRM, books the follow-up, and Slacks your rep. Different category."
"Send me some info first."
"Happy to. But I'll be real — generic info won't tell you anything useful, because what we build is custom to your business. The 15-minute call is the info. After it, you'll know exactly what we'd build for you, what it'd cost, and whether it's a fit. Thursday or Friday?"
"We're not ready for AI."
"Totally fair. Can I ask one question, just to know if I should call back in six months or never? What would have to be true for you to be ready?"
"Why not just hire a VA?"
"Great option for some things. A VA costs $4–5K a month, works 40 hours a week, takes vacations, and you're managing them. An AETHER agent costs less, works 168 hours a week, never quits, and we manage it. Different trade-off — VAs are better at judgment calls and relationship work, agents are better at speed and consistency. Most of our clients have both."
"What about data security?"
"Important question. Three things: the model provider, Anthropic, doesn't train on your data — that's contractual. Your agent runs in a dedicated cloud environment, not shared with other clients. And we maintain a SOC-2-ready posture with full audit logging on every action. Happy to share our security one-pager on the call."
"What if it makes a mistake?"
"Will happen occasionally — any AI does. So we design every agent with two safeguards: hard guardrails (it cannot do certain things without human approval — sending money, signing anything, contacting flagged accounts), and instant human escalation (if it's unsure, it stops and Slacks your team). The audit log shows every decision so you can spot patterns and tune."
"Send a proposal."
"Of course. I can't write a useful one without 15 minutes of context — every proposal we send is custom because every agent is custom. The call IS the discovery for the proposal. Worth Thursday or Friday?"
"I need to think about it."
"Makes sense. Before you go — what specifically would you want to think about? Sometimes I can answer it right now and save you a week."

Part 5 · Vertical playbooks

These are the seven verticals where AETHER closes fastest and delivers the most obvious ROI. For each: the pain you lead with, the agents to pitch, the ROI math, and a verbatim cold-call hook.

Law firms FLAGSHIP VERTICAL

This is your highest-margin, highest-retention vertical. Lawyers have measurable revenue per hour ($200–$600 billable), severe intake leakage, and budget. They also have strict requirements — which is good for us, because it raises the bar competitors can't clear.

The pain (use these stats on every call)

  • 42% of inbound law-firm calls go to voicemail or unanswered (Clio Legal Trends Report)
  • $187,000/year — average revenue an attorney loses to unbilled admin time
  • 15–25% — typical intake-to-signed-retainer conversion rate. Should be 40–50%.
  • 5–8 hours/week per attorney spent on case status update calls from existing clients

The four agents to pitch

  1. Intake Agent — every missed call or form-fill gets responded to within 90 seconds. Qualifies the matter (type, jurisdiction, statute timeline), runs a conflict check against Clio/MyCase/PracticePanther, books the consultation on the right attorney's calendar, opens the prospect file. This alone pays for the entire engagement.
  2. Status Update Agent — clients text "what's happening with my case?" → agent pulls latest matter notes + correspondence → sends accurate status (optionally for attorney review first). Eliminates the #1 time sink.
  3. Document Triage Agent — incoming docs (medical records, opposing counsel correspondence, court filings) get auto-categorized, tagged in case management, and routed to the responsible attorney.
  4. Follow-up Agent — leads that don't convert immediately get a 14-touch sequence over 90 days. Stops if they reply, books if they're ready, archives if they unsubscribe.

ROI math (do this on the call)

Solo practitioner · Spark ($997/mo) · recovers ~5 billable hrs/wk @ $300/hr = $6,000/mo recovered · 6× ROI
5-attorney firm · Catalyst ($2,997/mo) · 30% intake conversion lift on 60 leads/mo @ $4,500 avg matter = $27,000/mo new revenue · 9× ROI
15-attorney firm · Momentum ($7,997/mo) · all four agents · ~$60,000/mo recovered + new = 7.5× ROI
